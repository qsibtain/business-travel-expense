Carnegie Mellon University — Business Travel Expense
Per Diem Eligibility: This tool calculates per diem meal & incidentals for CMU employees on overnight business travel only. Non-employees must use actual receipts. Per diem and actual cost cannot be mixed on the same trip. Refer to the CMU Business Travel Expense Policy for full details.

Reimbursement Summary

Legend: Yellow = First/last travel day (75% rate)   Blue = Transition day between cities (use checkboxes to deduct meals not available)   Green = Extended stay (60% rate)

Incidentals are always reimbursed in full. Use the "Deduct" checkboxes to remove meals that were provided (conference, group meals) or not available due to travel timing.

Transition days: When two segments share the same date (e.g., depart City A and arrive City B on April 25), both rows appear — one labeled "departing" and one "arriving" — each using its own city's rates. Assign meals to the city where they were consumed. Incidentals are counted once (on the departing city row). To trigger this, set Segment 1's end date and Segment 2's start date to the same day.
